Hi! I am your
instructor

I am a Chilean journalist living and working in New York as a data journalism professor at the Craig Newmark Graduate School of Journalism at CUNY. Right now, I am teaching you "Fundamentals of Interactive & Data Journalism"

A little more about me

  • I founded Poderopedia, a site that maps who is who in business and politics in Latin America, and I previously worked in many news organizations in Chile.
  • Was a Nieman Berkman fellow at Harvard (2014-2015) and a Knight International Journalism fellow at ICFJ (2013-2014).
  • A few things I've worked in:
    1. The Ibero American Data Journalism Handbook.
    2. Map of Media and Media Ownership of Chile and Colombia.
    3. NarcoData, Animal Político, México.
    4. Justiciapedia, Chequeado, Argentina.
    5. EnData, El Nuevo Día, Puerto Rico.
    6. Dozens of workshops for reporters in 3 continents.

Three things I love

  1. Making barbecues for friends
  2. Reading, reporting and writing
  3. Helping you get new superpowers for your reporter toolbox
